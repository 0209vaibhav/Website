About Me

The world is my stage, and I am the storyteller, weaving poetry into the world through design. Every line a verse, every space a canvas, every story a path seeking its voice, every problem an opportunity to innovate, every interaction a rhythm in the symphony of design. Through the language of story, design, and tech, I bring unseen moments to life, weaving meaning into the tangible and the abstract.

I was born with the ability to design and impact the world around me. My journey began with architecture, shaping built spaces that define how people live and interact. It expanded into interior design, crafting environments that enrich everyday experiences, and into production design, framing cinematic worlds that tell compelling stories. Now, as a Computational Thinker, I merge design thinking with technology, algorithms, and spatial intelligence.

"I am an Architect and Computational Designer by mind, but a storyteller at heart."

I strive to discover the narratives woven into people's lives and the world around us, using them to develop interventions that are innovative yet poetic, driven by logic yet filled with soul. I seek to craft designs that make the world more intuitive, meaningful, and connected, ensuring that technology enhances human experience‚Äîwithout losing the beauty, rhythm, and poetry that make life extraordinary.

ODYSSEY 2.0 (WIP)

Transform Your Commute into interactive journeys

Created & Designed by Vaibhav Jain

In an era where technology shapes our routines and digital interactions often replace real-world experiences, we have become increasingly disconnected from our cities, the events unfolding around us, and even from one another.

The city is alive, yet we move through it passively. We walk the same paths every day, unaware of the vibrant moments happening around us. Street performances, a sudden snowfall, a hidden mural, a spontaneous community gathering‚Äîthese fleeting experiences appear and disappear, undocumented, unshared, and unnoticed.

Odyssey 2.0 bridges this disconnect. It is a map created by the people, for the people‚Äîa platform designed to connect individuals with each other and their city. A live, interactive city map, Odyssey 2.0 captures and shares ephemeral urban moments‚Äîevents, activities, and experiences that cannot be found on traditional navigation apps.

A community-driven platform, Odyssey 2.0 empowers users to broadcast and discover spontaneous city happenings, from a street musician's melody to an impromptu art installation or a breathtaking sunset at an overlooked viewpoint. Unlike static mapping services, Odyssey 2.0 creates a dynamic, real-time city experience‚Äîa space where people don't just navigate, but actively engage. By transforming everyday commutes into immersive urban explorations, it shifts technology from a barrier to a bridge, reconnecting people with their surroundings, their city, and each other.

Sidewalk Reclamation: Computing Natural Interventions

Computational Modeling

By Luc Wilson, Meli Harvey - Summer 2024

A data-driven approach to analyze sidewalk encroachment patterns and propose strategic natural interventions. The system analyses the radiation, sunlight and claustrophic analysis results on the sidewalks to understand the peak radiation points to generate targeted solutions using green buffers and natural barriers, enhancing both walkability and urban ecology.

Hue Knew: Interactive Film Recognition Game

Data Visualization for Architecture, Urbanism and the Humanities (Audit)

By Jia Zhang - Fall 2024

Hue Knew is an innovative interactive quiz game that challenges players to identify films through blurred stills and posters. Players must deduce the movie's genre, release year, and title from visually abstracted content. Leveraging The Movie Database (TMDB) API for real-time image and metadata retrieval, the game offers a continuously fresh challenge. The integrated data visualization dashboard tracks player performance metrics, revealing patterns in film recognition abilities and genre-specific trends, making learning about cinema both engaging and analytical.
